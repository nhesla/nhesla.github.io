Featured Project

Unreal Engine 4 C++ 8 Weeks Team of 8
Tasty Pastries Capstone · Lead Programmer
The Situation

Eight-week capstone. Three programmers, five artists — and a lead who burned three weeks trying to get VR working before leaving the project entirely. By week three, nothing was built. I stepped into the lead role and started rebuilding from scratch.

Technical Decisions
  • Pivoted from VR to first-person to eliminate the player model/animation problem and keep scope tight
  • Built a generic "usable object" base class — pickups, interactions, and ingredient logic all inherited from a single system, so artists could swap in new meshes with minimal programmer involvement
  • Designed the recipe-matching system around ingredient state tracking in the mixing bowl, with flexible tolerance so imprecise amounts still registered correctly
  • Identified that pairing my other programmer with my own in-progress work created bottlenecks — restructured so he owned separate systems end-to-end (the recipe board, customer logic) rather than extending mine
What I Owned
  • Full core gameplay loop: object pickup, ingredient combination, recipe validation, level progression
  • Art pipeline QA — caught and standardized mesh scale, pivot, collision, and texture issues across all imported assets
  • Build debugging on presentation morning: tracked down and fixed a build-only bug with no runtime equivalent under a hard time deadline
Outcome

Shipped on deadline. Came back from three weeks behind in a ten-week course and earned a B. The audience response — kids genuinely engaged with it — was the part I didn't see coming and made the grind worth it.
